2. Getting Started

This section will guide you through creating an account, verifying your email, and understanding the role approval process.

2.1 Creating an Account

1 Navigate to Registration

Click "Login/Register" in the sidebar navigation, then select the "Register" link below the login form.

2 Fill in Your Details

Provide the following information:

  • Full Name: Your complete name as it should appear in the system
  • Username: Choose a unique username (will be checked in real-time)
  • Email: A valid email address for account verification
  • Password: A secure password (strength meter will guide you)
3 Select Your Role

Choose the role that best describes your function. Each role has different permissions and requirements. See Section 3 for detailed role descriptions.

Note: If you select a restricted role (Field Collector, Analyst, Lab Partner, Industry Partner, Government Official, Site Manager), you may need to provide organization details and wait for admin approval before gaining full access.
4 Submit Registration

Review your information and click "Register". You'll be redirected to a confirmation page.

2.2 Email Verification

After registration, a verification email will be sent to your registered email address. This email contains a unique link that expires in 24 hours.

1 Check Your Email

Look for an email from Jal Sarovar with the subject "Verify Your Email Address".

2 Click Verification Link

Click the verification link in the email. You'll be redirected to the Jal Sarovar platform with a confirmation message.

Important: You cannot log in until your email is verified. If you don't receive the email within 10 minutes, check your spam folder or request a new verification email.

2.3 Role Approval Process

Public roles (Viewer, Citizen Contributor, Researcher) are automatically approved upon email verification. Restricted roles require admin review and approval.

Automatic Approval (Public Roles)

  • Viewer: Immediate access after email verification
  • Citizen Contributor: Immediate access after email verification
  • Researcher: Immediate access after email verification

Manual Approval (Restricted Roles)

If you requested a restricted role, the following process applies:

  1. Pending Status: Your account is created with "Viewer" permissions and marked as "Pending Approval"
  2. Admin Review: System administrators review your request (typically within 24-48 hours)
  3. Email Notification: You'll receive an email when your role is approved or if additional information is needed
  4. Full Access: Once approved, you can log in with full permissions for your role

3. User Roles & Permissions

Jal Sarovar uses a role-based access control system with 10 distinct user roles. Each role has specific permissions designed to match real-world responsibilities in water quality monitoring.

Permission Matrix

Role Descriptions

Public Roles (Auto-Approved)

  • Viewer: Read-only access to public water quality data. Ideal for citizens, students, and general public interest.
  • Citizen Contributor: Can submit visual observations and photos of water bodies. Helps crowdsource water quality monitoring.
  • Researcher: Access all datasets for research purposes. Can submit samples but not test results. Ideal for academic researchers.

Restricted Roles (Requires Approval)

  • Field Collector: Collects water samples from assigned sites. Records physical observations. Cannot submit lab results.
  • Analyst: Data scientist or analyst role. Access to ML models, analytics dashboards, and API endpoints.
  • Lab Partner: Laboratory personnel who submit test results. Requires organization credentials. Can bulk import data.
  • Industry Partner: Industry compliance monitoring for assigned sites. Submit test results and manage interventions.
  • Government Official: Government agency personnel. Full access to create sites, submit data, access reports.
  • Site Manager: Manages specific water body sites. Can create/edit site info and submit samples.

System Roles

  • Admin: Full system access including user management, role approvals, and system settings.

5. Managing Water Body Sites

5.1 Viewing Sites

Navigate to /sites from the sidebar menu to view all water body monitoring sites.

Filtering Sites

Use the filter controls to narrow down the site list:

  • Country: Filter by country (defaults to India)
  • State: Select specific state (cascading filter)
  • Site Type: Pond, Lake, Tank, Reservoir, River, Stepwell, etc.
  • Category: Public or Residential
  • Risk Level: Critical, High, Medium, Low (based on ML predictions)

Site Information Displayed

Each site card shows:

  • Site name and location (state, district)
  • Risk level badge with color coding
  • Latest WQI score
  • Number of samples collected
  • Contamination status
  • Quick action buttons (View Details, Add Sample)

5.2 Creating a New Site

Required Role: Admin, Government Official, or Site Manager

1 Click "New Site" Button

Navigate to /sites and click the "New Site" button in the top-right corner.

2 Fill in Site Identification
  • Site Code: Unique identifier (auto-generated or custom)
  • Site Name: Descriptive name of the water body
  • AMRIT Sarovar ID: Official Mission Amrit Sarovar identifier (if applicable)
3 Enter Location Details
  • Country: Select India (default)
  • State: Choose from dropdown
  • District: Select district within state
  • Block/Village: More specific location
  • Coordinates: Latitude and Longitude (decimal degrees)
4 Select Site Type and Category
  • Site Type: Pond, Lake, Tank, Reservoir, River, Stepwell, etc.
  • Category: Public (monitored by government) or Residential (private households)
5 Add Infrastructure Details
  • Surface Area: In hectares
  • Water Source: Groundwater, rainwater, surface water, etc.
  • Population Served: Estimated number of people
  • Environmental Factors: Coastal, Industrial Nearby, Agricultural Nearby, Urban Area
6 Submit Site

Review all information and click "Create Site". The site will be added to the monitoring system.

5.3 Editing Site Information

Required Role: Admin, Government Official, or Site Manager (for assigned sites)

To edit an existing site:

  1. Navigate to the site detail page
  2. Click the "Edit Site" button
  3. Update the necessary fields
  4. Click "Save Changes"
Note: Changing site coordinates or type may trigger automatic ML model re-evaluation. Risk predictions and recommendations may be updated.

6. Submitting Water Samples

Required Role: Citizen Contributor (observations only), Researcher, Field Collector, Analyst, Lab Partner, Industry Partner, Government Official, Site Manager, or Admin

6.1 Sample Collection Workflow

1 Navigate to Sample Creation

Go to /samples and click "New Sample" or click "Add Sample" from a site detail page.

2 Select Site

Choose the water body site where the sample was collected. Sites are filtered based on your permissions.

3 Enter Collection Metadata
  • Collection Date: Date sample was collected (YYYY-MM-DD format)
  • Collection Time: Time of day (HH:MM)
  • Source Point: Specific location within water body (e.g., "North Shore", "Inlet")
  • Depth: Sampling depth in meters (surface, mid-depth, bottom)
  • Collected By: Name of person collecting the sample

6.2 Recording Observations

4 Weather Conditions
  • Weather: Sunny, Cloudy, Rainy, Windy
  • Recent Rainfall: Yes/No and amount if known
  • Air Temperature: Ambient temperature in °C
5 Visual Observations
  • Color: Colorless, Yellowish, Brownish, Greenish, etc.
  • Odor: None, Chlorinous, Musty, Fishy, etc.
  • Turbidity (Visual): Clear, Slightly Turbid, Very Turbid
  • Algae Presence: None, Light, Moderate, Heavy
  • Floating Matter: None, Leaves, Debris, Oil Film, etc.
6 Submit Sample Record

Click "Submit Sample". The sample will be created with status "Collected" and await lab test results.

What Happens Next: The sample appears in the system with "Awaiting Testing" status. Lab partners can now submit test results for this sample. Once test results are submitted, the ML pipeline automatically analyzes the data and generates predictions.

8. Understanding ML Reports

Jal Sarovar uses 6 machine learning models to analyze water quality data and provide actionable insights. This section explains how to interpret ML-generated reports.

8.1 Site Risk Classification

Model: Random Forest Classifier

Purpose: Predict the contamination risk level of a water body site

Risk Levels

  • Critical: Immediate action required. High probability of contamination. Test weekly.
  • High: Elevated risk. Monitor closely. Test bi-weekly.
  • Medium: Moderate risk. Regular monitoring recommended. Test monthly.
  • Low: Minimal risk. Standard monitoring adequate. Test quarterly.

How to Interpret

Risk predictions are based on:

  • Site type and environmental factors
  • Historical contamination patterns
  • Proximity to industrial/agricultural areas
  • Population density served
  • Water source characteristics
Recommended Actions:
  • Critical Sites: Conduct immediate testing, consider water treatment interventions
  • High Risk Sites: Increase testing frequency, monitor for changes
  • Medium/Low Risk: Maintain regular testing schedule

8.2 Contamination Detection

Model: XGBoost Classifier with SHAP Explanations

Purpose: Identify the type of contamination present in water samples

Contamination Types

  • Runoff & Sediment: Agricultural or urban runoff carrying sediments and nutrients
  • Sewage Ingress: Fecal contamination from sewage leaks or overflows
  • Salt Intrusion: Saline water contamination (coastal areas or brackish sources)
  • Pipe Corrosion: Metal contamination from corroding distribution pipes
  • Disinfectant Decay: Chlorine degradation allowing bacterial growth

Confidence Scores

Each prediction includes a confidence percentage (0-100%):

  • > 80%: High confidence - Act on this prediction
  • 60-80%: Moderate confidence - Consider additional testing
  • < 60%: Low confidence - More data needed

8.3 Water Quality Index (WQI)

Method: Penalty Scoring Algorithm (Rule-Based)

Purpose: Provide a single numeric score representing overall water quality

WQI Score Ranges

WQI Score Classification Drinkability Description
90-100 Excellent Safe Suitable for drinking without treatment
70-89 Compliant Safe Meets WHO/BIS standards, safe for drinking
50-69 Warning Marginal Some parameters exceed limits, treatment recommended
0-49 Unsafe Not Safe Not suitable for drinking, treatment required

How WQI is Calculated

The WQI uses a penalty scoring method:

  1. Each parameter is compared to WHO/BIS standards
  2. Exceedances are assigned penalty points based on severity
  3. Total penalties are subtracted from 100
  4. Critical parameters (e.g., E. coli, lead) have higher penalty weights

8.4 Forecasting & Predictions

Model: Gaussian Process Regression

Purpose: Predict future parameter values and identify degradation trends

Forecast Outputs

  • Predicted Value: Expected parameter level in 30/60/90 days
  • 95% Confidence Interval: Range of likely values (uncertainty quantification)
  • Threshold Exceedance Probability: Likelihood of exceeding WHO/BIS limits
  • Days Until Exceedance: Estimated time before parameter becomes unsafe

Common Forecasted Parameters

  • pH: Track acidification or alkalinization trends
  • Turbidity: Predict seasonal changes in water clarity
  • TDS: Monitor mineral content accumulation
  • Free Chlorine: Forecast disinfectant decay in distribution systems

10. Intervention Management

Required Role: Analyst, Industry Partner, Government Official, Site Manager, or Admin

Interventions are water treatment actions taken to improve water quality. The system tracks intervention effectiveness by comparing water quality before and after treatment.

Creating an Intervention

  1. Navigate to /interventions
  2. Click "New Intervention"
  3. Select the site
  4. Choose intervention type (Chemical Treatment, Filtration, Disinfection, Infrastructure Repair, etc.)
  5. Record start date and estimated cost
  6. Document treatment details
  7. Submit intervention

Tracking Effectiveness

The system automatically:

  • Compares WQI scores before and after intervention
  • Calculates percentage improvement
  • Tracks cost-effectiveness (improvement per rupee spent)
  • Generates intervention effectiveness reports

11. Bulk Data Import

Required Role: Lab Partner, Industry Partner, Government Official, or Admin

Bulk import allows uploading multiple samples and test results via CSV files, ideal for laboratories submitting batch results.

CSV Import Process

1 Download CSV Template

Navigate to /imports/history and click "Download Template". Choose the appropriate template (Sites, Samples, or Test Results).

2 Fill in Your Data

Open the template in Excel or Google Sheets. Fill in your data following the column headers exactly.

3 Upload CSV File

Click "New Import", select your CSV file, and choose the import type.

4 Review Validation

The system validates your data and shows any errors. Fix errors and re-upload if needed.

5 Confirm Import

Once validation passes, confirm the import. Data will be added to the system.

Important: Bulk imports trigger ML analysis for all samples. Large imports (>100 samples) may take several minutes to process.

13. Frequently Asked Questions

How long does role approval take?

Answer: Role approvals for restricted roles are typically processed within 24-48 hours. Admins review requests during business hours. You'll receive an email notification once your role is approved or if additional information is needed.

What if I forget my password?

Answer: Click "Forgot Password" on the login page. Enter your registered email address. You'll receive a password reset link valid for 2 hours. Click the link and set a new password.

Can I submit data without lab test results?

Answer: Yes! Citizen Contributors can submit visual observations and photos. Field Collectors can create sample records with physical observations. Lab test results are submitted separately by Lab Partners.

How accurate are the ML predictions?

Answer: Model accuracy varies by type:

  • Site Risk Classification: 84.6% accuracy
  • Contamination Type Detection: 87.3% accuracy
  • WQI Calculation: ±10 points MAE (Mean Absolute Error)
  • Time-series Forecasting: R² = 0.94 (very high correlation)
All predictions include confidence scores to help you assess reliability.

Who can see my submitted data?

Answer: Data visibility depends on site category:

  • Public Sites: All authenticated users can view (read-only for most roles)
  • Residential Sites: Only assigned users and admins can view
Personal information (your name, email) is never shared publicly. Only site and sample data is visible.

How often should we test water quality?

Answer: Testing frequency depends on risk level (based on ML predictions):

  • Critical Risk: Weekly testing recommended
  • High Risk: Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)
  • Medium Risk: Monthly
  • Low Risk: Quarterly (every 3 months)
The Cost Optimizer model provides customized recommendations for each site.

Can I export data for my own analysis?

Answer: Yes! Users with appropriate permissions can export data in CSV or JSON format. Navigate to the Sites or Samples page and click the "Export" button. Researchers have access to bulk export functionality.

What do I do if I find incorrect data?

Answer: Contact your admin or use the "Report Issue" feature on the specific sample or site page. Lab Partners can edit their own submitted test results. Admins can correct or delete erroneous data.

Is there a mobile app?

Answer: The web application is mobile-responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. A dedicated mobile app for Android/iOS is planned for future releases to support offline sample collection.

14. Glossary of Terms

Term Definition
WQI Water Quality Index - A single numeric score (0-100) representing overall water quality
TDS Total Dissolved Solids - Measure of dissolved mineral content in water (mg/L)
BOD Biochemical Oxygen Demand - Amount of oxygen consumed by bacteria decomposing organic matter (mg/L)
COD Chemical Oxygen Demand - Measure of organic pollution (mg/L)
NTU Nephelometric Turbidity Units - Measure of water cloudiness/clarity
CFU Colony Forming Units - Measure of viable bacterial cells in microbiology
MPN Most Probable Number - Statistical method for estimating bacterial concentration
WHO World Health Organization - Sets international water quality standards
BIS Bureau of Indian Standards - India's national standards body (IS 10500:2012 for drinking water)
LEGOLAS Low-cost Environmental Geospatial Observation and Laboratory Aquatic System - Autonomous robotic platform for water sampling
ML Machine Learning - Algorithms that learn patterns from data to make predictions
SHAP SHapley Additive exPlanations - Method for explaining ML model predictions
R² R-squared - Statistical measure of how well predictions fit actual data (0-1, higher is better)
MAE Mean Absolute Error - Average prediction error magnitude
CUSUM Cumulative Sum - Statistical method for detecting changes in data over time
Amrit Sarovar Government of India mission to conserve and develop 68,000+ water bodies nationwide
Jal Jeevan Mission Government initiative to provide safe drinking water to all rural households
Site A water body monitoring location (pond, lake, tank, river, etc.)
Sample A water specimen collected at a specific date/time from a site
Test Result Laboratory analysis results for water quality parameters
Intervention Water treatment action taken to improve quality (filtration, disinfection, etc.)
